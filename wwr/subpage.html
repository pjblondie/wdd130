Rivers

Oregan Rivers

Oregon boasts a wide variety of rivers that cut through forests, lava fields, and high desert. The Deschutes River is a popular choice, offering consistent rapids and year-round rafting just outside of Bend. The Rogue River, one of the original Wild and Scenic Rivers, winds through rugged canyons with legendary whitewater and rich history. Whether you're paddling past waterfalls or floating beneath old-growth forests, Oregon rivers are a paddler's paradise.

Montana Rivers

Montana's rivers are known for their wild beauty and pristine waters. The Flathead River offers crystal-clear water fed by Glacier National Park, with a mix of gentle sections and technical rapids. The Yellowstone River is the longest free-flowing river in the lower 48, offering sweeping views of mountain valleys and abundant wildlife. For those who want an unforgettable backcountry adventure, Montana's rivers provide the perfect blend of solitude and excitement.

Utah Rivers

Utah is home to some of the most iconic desert river landscapes in the country. The Colorado River winds through deep red rock canyons, offering world-class rafting in places like Cataract Canyon. The Green River, a favorite among rafters, flows through Dinosaur National Monument and Desolation Canyon, providing both thrilling rapids and peaceful stretches surrounded by towering cliffs. Whether you're looking for heart-pounding adventure or scenic floats, Utah rivers deliver a one-of-a-kind experience.
